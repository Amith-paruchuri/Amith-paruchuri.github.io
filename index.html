Research Projects

ECG-Derived Cardiac Biological Age Gap (AHA 2026)

A 1D residual neural network was applied to the PTB-XL dataset (n=21,837 ECGs) to predict chronological age from raw 12-lead waveforms, enabling computation of an ECG biological age gap for each subject. Age-adjusted gap analysis across diagnostic categories revealed significantly elevated gaps in conduction disorders, ST/T-wave changes, and myocardial infarction versus normal ECGs.

Sex-stratified analysis showed females consistently demonstrated larger positive age-adjusted gaps than males across all pathological categories. Per-lead waveform feature analysis identified lead skewness — particularly V2 skewness (r=0.350) and aVL skewness (r=0.342) — as the dominant morphological substrate of cardiac electrical aging, outperforming amplitude-based features. These findings support integration of ECG biological age gap within multi-organ biological age frameworks alongside neural and retinal aging biomarkers.

Tools: Python, PyTorch, 1D ResNet, SciPy, Pandas

Transcriptomic Drivers of MASLD Progression: Osteopontin (SPP1) as a Central Network Hub

RNA-sequencing data from 216 liver biopsies (control, NAFL, and NASH stratified by fibrosis stage F0–F4) were analyzed to identify the transcriptional programs driving progression from steatosis to steatohepatitis. Differential expression using voom-limma identified a 563-gene progression signature (493 upregulated, 70 downregulated; FDR <0.05), with upregulation of fibroinflammatory genes and suppression of metabolic programs.

GSEA revealed enrichment of EMT, TNF/NF-κB, IL-6/STAT3, and inflammatory pathways. Network analysis of the top DEGs mapped onto STRING identified SPP1 (osteopontin) as the dominant hub gene (betweenness 476, over double the next node), centrally connecting inflammatory, matrix-remodeling, and ductular modules. SPP1 rose progressively with fibrosis stage (FDR 2.9e-9), identifying it as a candidate biomarker and therapeutic target for noninvasive MASLD staging.

Tools: R, voom-limma, GSEA, clusterProfiler, STRING network analysis

Brain–Retina Biological Aging Coupling

This project investigates whether biological aging is reflected systemically across different organs by studying the coupling between retinal and brain aging signatures. Independent aging clocks were developed from retinal fundus imaging using pretrained foundation models and from structural brain imaging features. The core objective was to determine whether deviations from chronological age in these two modalities converge or diverge across the lifespan.

The analysis involved residualized modeling, interaction analysis, and spline-based nonlinear regression to examine cross-modal relationships while accounting for age effects. This work demonstrates how multimodal imaging can capture shared aging biology.

Tools: Python, PyTorch, NumPy, Pandas, SciPy, Matplotlib

Astrocyte Transcriptomic Signatures in Alzheimer's Disease and Tauopathies

Using single-nucleus RNA sequencing, this study identifies shared and disease-specific astrocyte states across Alzheimer's disease and primary tauopathies. Clustering and differential proportion analysis revealed distinct astrocyte phenotypes enriched in inflammatory, stress signaling, and homeostatic pathways.

Pathway enrichment and spatial transcriptomic mapping were used to localize these astrocyte states within brain regions, enabling identification of reactive phenotypes that differ across disease proteinopathies.

Tools: R, Seurat, SCENIC, clusterProfiler

Lymphocyte Infiltration at Tumor Border in DLBCL

This computational pathology project quantifies how lymphocytes infiltrate tumor borders in DLBCL whole-slide images. Tumor and cellular compartments were segmented using deep learning, and a transition zone band was created around tumor margins.

Spatial infiltration patterns were modeled as continuous curves and slope-based features were extracted to objectively represent tumor–immune topology. These features capture biologically meaningful spatial heterogeneity not visible through bulk immune density.

Tools: Python, TIAToolbox, CellViT, OpenCV

AML Transcriptomic Genomic Risk Score

This work develops a transcriptome-derived genomic risk score capable of capturing immune regulatory and leukocyte differentiation programs beyond traditional cytogenetic risk stratification. Survival modeling and LASSO regression were used to derive gene signatures.

Differential expression and pathway enrichment analyses were used to characterize immune signaling landscapes underlying adverse prognosis.

Tools: R, limma, glmnet, survival, clusterProfiler

Immune Activation Index in Coeliac Disease

This study investigates mucosal transcriptomic landscapes in coeliac disease using differential gene expression and immune deconvolution. An immune activation index was derived to quantify mucosal immune state beyond histology.

Pathway enrichment highlighted inflammatory signaling, antigen presentation, and lymphoid infiltration correlating with disease activity.

Tools: R, limma, xCell, clusterProfiler

Dementia Stage Classification from MRI using MobileNet

Transfer learning using MobileNet was applied to structural MRI to classify stages of dementia. Grad-CAM visualization was used to confirm spatial consistency of attention with disease- relevant anatomical regions.

Tools: Python, PyTorch, Grad-CAM

EEG Spectrogram Classification for Schizophrenia

Mitotic Cell Detection using SAM + ResNet

Mitotic figures are critical prognostic markers in several tumors. In this project, RGB crops around cell coordinates were generated and fused with SAM-derived segmentation masks to train a ResNet classifier for mitotic detection.

Tools: Python, OpenCV, SAM, ResNet
